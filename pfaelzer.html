      • Morris PFAELZER (b. 1913, d. 1980s) first married Marjorie LESSER. Remarried Marianna (b. 1926 Los Angeles, California). Marianna She received an A.B. from the University of California, Santa Barbara in 1949. She received a J.D. from the UCLA School of Law in 1957. She was in private practice in Los Angeles, California from 1957 to 1978. PFAELZER was a federal judge on the United States District Court for the Central District of California. PFAELZER was nominated by President Jimmy CARTER on August 8, 1978, to a seat vacated by Francis C. WHELAN. She was confirmed by the United States Senate on September 22, 1978, and received commission on September 23, 1978. She was the first female District Judge appointed to that District. She assumed senior status on December 31, 1997. She is noted for her role in striking down California's Proposition 187, which would have denied services to illegal aliens.
